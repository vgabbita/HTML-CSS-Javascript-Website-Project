What is VEX?

VEX is an educational robotics platform that is commonly used in schools and other similar institutions for its ease of use and flexibility. Students and educators use VEX hardware, such as metal, sensors, and screws, to assemble robots, which are then programmed to complete tasks.

At the competitive level, teams of students use VEX parts to construct robots that can perform certain tasks to score points in a game. This year's game is VRC Spin Up, which includes elements of disc golf. Robots can launch small, frisbee-like discs into goals, and there are also other field elements that can be manipulated and actions that can be taken to earn points. High-performing robots will need to be well-constructed and well-programmed in order to compete in a very fast-paced and competitive environment.

About High Tech's VEX Teams

765 FuryTech is the competitive VEX organization of High Technology High School, a public, pre-engineering, vocational high school in Lincroft, New Jersey. High Tech has three VEX teams – 765A, 765X, and 765N – that HTHS students can apply to compete on. Generally, the HTHS VEX teams are high-performing in New Jersey competitions, and the teams have attended the VEX Robotics World Championship – the highest level of VEX competition – multiple times since their founding, including most recently in May 2022.
